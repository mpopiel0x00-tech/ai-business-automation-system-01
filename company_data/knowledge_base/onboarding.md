This guide explains how a new company can start using FlowForge to manage workflows and automate operations.

---

## Step 1: Create Your Workspace

When signing up for FlowForge, your company creates a workspace.

The workspace is the central environment where teams manage projects, workflows, and automation pipelines.

During workspace creation you will:

- choose a workspace name
- invite team members
- configure basic settings

---

## Step 2: Invite Your Team

FlowForge allows you to invite teammates by email.

Each user will receive an invitation link that allows them to join the workspace.

You can assign roles such as:

- Admin
- Manager
- Member
- Viewer

This ensures that team members have the appropriate level of access.

---

## Step 3: Create Your First Project

Projects allow teams to organize work around specific initiatives.

Examples include:

- product development
- marketing campaigns
- customer onboarding
- internal operations

Within each project you can create task boards and workflows.

---

## Step 4: Configure Task Boards

Task boards allow teams to visualize and manage work.

Typical columns include:

- Backlog
- In Progress
- Review
- Completed

Tasks can be assigned to team members, given deadlines, and tracked as they move through the workflow.

---

## Step 5: Set Up Automations

Once your workflow structure is established, you can begin creating automation rules.

Examples include:

- automatically assigning tasks
- sending notifications for deadlines
- updating project status based on task changes

Automation rules reduce manual coordination and improve workflow consistency.

---

## Step 6: Connect Integrations

To maximize efficiency, teams can connect FlowForge with external tools such as Slack, GitHub, and Google Workspace.

Integrations allow FlowForge workflows to interact with your existing systems.

---

## Step 7: Monitor Progress with Analytics

After workflows are active, the analytics dashboard provides insights into team productivity and workflow performance.

Managers can monitor project progress, identify bottlenecks, and improve operational efficiency.

---

Following these steps helps teams adopt FlowForge quickly while establishing structured workflows that scale as the organization grows.
